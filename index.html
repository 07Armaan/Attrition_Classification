About the project

A second opinion, built on data instead of gut feel.

Most attrition is predictable in hindsight — pay fell behind the market, promotions stalled, overtime crept up, recognition dried up. Attrition Insight takes the same eighteen signals an experienced HR partner would look at and turns them into a single, consistent probability, every time, for every employee.

  • Trained on structured employee records — compensation, role, tenure, satisfaction, and work conditions.
  • Outputs a calibrated probability, not just a label, so you can prioritise by severity.
  • Ships as a lightweight web form backed by a FastAPI service

How it works

From a profile to a prediction in four steps.

The same pipeline runs every time, so results stay consistent across employees, teams, and review cycles.

01

You fill in the employee profile

Nineteen fields covering demographics, role, compensation, satisfaction, and work conditions — the same fields HR systems already track. No file upload, no spreadsheet required.

02

The form encodes it for the model

Categorical answers like job role or work-life balance are converted into the exact numeric format the model was trained on, then bundled into a single request payload.

03

FastAPI calls the trained model

The payload is sent to a /predict endpoint, where your serialized classifier (scikit-learn, XGBoost, or similar) scores it in milliseconds.

04

You get a probability, not a guess

The result comes back as a percentage risk of attrition plus a Stay / Leave verdict, rendered live on the predictor page with no page reload.

The model

A binary classifier, trained to spot the early signal.

Attrition Insight is model-agnostic. The interface and API contract are built around a standard tabular classification setup: nineteen input features, one binary target — Stayed or Left.

  • Works with scikit-learn, XGBoost, LightGBM, or any model exposing a predict_proba()-style method.
  • Categorical fields are one-hot or ordinal encoded server-side to match your training pipeline.
  • Swap the serialized model file without touching the frontend — the form and API contract stay fixed.

Definitions

Every field, in plain language.

A quick reference for each input the predictor asks for, and what it means in the model.

Attrition
The rate at which employees leave a company, voluntarily or otherwise, over a given period. The model predicts the probability an individual employee falls into the "left" group.
Work-Life Balance
Self-reported balance between job demands and personal life, captured as Excellent, Good, Fair, or Poor.
Job Satisfaction
How content the employee is in their current role, rated Very High, High, Medium, or Low.
Performance Rating
The employee's most recent formal performance review outcome: High, Average, Below Average, or Low.
Job Level
Seniority tier within their role — Entry, Mid, or Senior.
Company Tenure
Total years the employee has been with the organisation across all roles, distinct from years in their current position.
Leadership Opportunities
Whether the employee currently has access to leadership-track responsibilities or mentoring programmes (Yes / No).
Innovation Opportunities
Whether the employee's role allows room to propose, build, or experiment with new ideas (Yes / No).
Company Reputation
The employee's perception of how the company is viewed externally — Excellent, Good, Fair, or Poor.
Employee Recognition
How recognised the employee feels for their contributions, rated Very High, High, Medium, or Low.

Why it matters

Replacing an employee costs far more than keeping one.

Recruiting, onboarding, and lost productivity stack up quietly. Catching risk early turns an exit into a conversation.

FAQ

Good to know before you predict.

Is this connected to a real machine learning model?
Yes. The form sends data to a FastAPI /predict endpoint. Out of the box it ships with a placeholder scoring function so the whole flow works end to end — swap in your trained model file and it scores real predictions immediately.
What format does my model need to be in?
Any model saved as a .pkl or .joblib file that exposes a scikit-learn-style predict_proba() method works out of the box with the provided backend code. Other formats just need a small loader adjustment.
Do I need to change the encoding of categorical fields?
The backend includes an encoding map matching the exact category options used in the form. If your training pipeline encoded categories differently (e.g. a different one-hot column order), update the encoding section in main.py to match.
Can this run without internet access, fully self-hosted?
Yes. Both pages are static HTML/CSS/JS with no external dependencies beyond a Google Fonts import, and the backend is a self-contained FastAPI app — deploy both on your own infrastructure.
Is employee data stored anywhere?
Not by default. The reference backend scores each request and returns a result without persisting it. Add your own logging or database layer if you need an audit trail.
